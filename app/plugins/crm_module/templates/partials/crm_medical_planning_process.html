Assessment before a binding quote (MNA & plan)

Reputable medical event providers usually treat a medical needs assessment and an event medical plan as part of proper governance before committing to cover. Early numbers from this form can start a conversation; a final quote follows the full risk profile and agreed scope.

Why the plan matters for pricing

  • Risk-led, not attendance-only: guidance such as the Purple Guide frames provision around risk; a smaller high-risk event can need more resource than a larger low-risk one.
  • Defining scope: staffing (e.g. doctors, paramedics, first aiders), equipment, vehicles, and facilities are priced once required levels are agreed.
  • Compliance: providers typically match planned cover to registration and regulatory expectations (e.g. CQC in England where applicable) and local multi-agency expectations.

Typical sequence

  1. Discussion: event type, audience, history, and context (this enquiry captures high-level inputs).
  2. Assessment: medical needs assessment to agree the required level of cover.
  3. Quote: formal proposal based on that assessment.
  4. After acceptance: detailed event medical plan, often shared with the Safety Advisory Group (SAG) or equivalent.

Takeaway: quoting without a proper needs assessment risks weak cover or wasted cost. Numbers here are indicative for early conversation, not a substitute for your own risk assessment or clinical sign-off.

Medical needs assessment & event medical plan

Standard practice for medical event cover: a medical needs assessment and event medical plan underpin governance before a binding quote. Early bands and headcounts here support pipeline and rough estimates only; final pricing follows agreed scope (staffing grades, kit, vehicles, facilities).

Why plan-led quoting

  • Risk-led (e.g. Purple Guide framing): not attendance alone.
  • Scope for accurate fees: roles, equipment, vehicles, infrastructure.
  • Compliance: e.g. CQC / registration and SAG-aligned documentation.

Typical flow

  1. Discussion & context
  2. Medical needs assessment
  3. Quote from agreed requirements
  4. After award: final event medical plan (often SAG)

Takeaway: avoid binding prices from guide numbers alone — under- or over-resourcing both harm the organiser. Use CRM event plans and quote revisions once assessment is recorded.
